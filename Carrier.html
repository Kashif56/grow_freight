COMMON QUESTIONS

Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about partnering with GrowMore Freight as a carrier.

To join our network, carriers must have active operating authority, meet our insurance requirements (minimum $1M auto liability, $100K cargo), maintain a satisfactory safety rating, and provide required documentation including a W-9, certificates of insurance, and operating authority. We also consider your safety record, service history, and equipment specifications during the approval process.

Our standard payment terms are Net 30 days from receipt of complete and accurate documentation. We also offer a Quick Pay program that provides payment within 48-72 hours for a small fee (typically 2-3%). Electronic documentation submission through our portal can significantly speed up the payment process by eliminating mail delays and ensuring all required documents are properly submitted.

Once approved as a carrier, you'll have access to our online portal where you can view available loads matching your equipment type and preferred lanes. You can also receive load offers directly from our carrier representatives via phone, email, or text. Our system considers your location, equipment type, and preferences to suggest the most suitable loads. You can negotiate rates and accept loads directly through the portal or with your carrier representative.

We work with a diverse range of freight types including dry van, refrigerated, flatbed, step deck, RGN, oversized loads, and specialized equipment. Our shipper base spans multiple industries including manufacturing, retail, food and beverage, construction, and agriculture. This diversity allows us to offer a wide variety of loads to match different equipment types and carrier preferences.

We maintain a 24/7 support team to address any issues that arise during transit. If you encounter problems such as delays, facility issues, or mechanical breakdowns, contact your assigned representative or our emergency support line immediately. We work collaboratively with carriers and shippers to find practical solutions rather than assigning blame. Our goal is to maintain strong relationships with both carriers and shippers by addressing issues promptly and fairly.
